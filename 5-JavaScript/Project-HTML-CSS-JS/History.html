History

## 📽️ Film and Television Institute of Hyderabad (FTIH): A Deep History
### 🌱 Origins: The Multimedia Spark (2007–2010)
The Film and Television Institute of Hyderabad (FTIH) began its journey in **2007**, not as a film school, but as a modest multimedia education center under the name **Impart Multimedia Education**. Founded in Hyderabad, the institute was born from a simple yet powerful idea: to make **creative and technical education accessible** to passionate learners, regardless of their background.
At the time, Hyderabad was emerging as a hub for animation and digital design, and FTIH capitalized on this momentum by offering courses in **graphic design, web development, animation, and video editing**. The founders believed that the future of storytelling lay not just in traditional cinema, but in the convergence of digital media and visual arts.
### 🎬 Transformation into a Film School (2010–2015)
As demand for film-related education grew, the institute’s leadership—led by visionary minds like **Mr. Uday Kiran Katakam**—recognized the need to expand beyond multimedia. In **2010**, the board made a pivotal decision: to evolve into a full-fledged film and television institute. Thus, **FTIH** was born.
This transformation wasn’t just a rebranding—it was a philosophical shift. FTIH began offering **professional courses in direction, cinematography, acting, anchoring, and scriptwriting**, alongside its technical programs. The curriculum was redesigned to blend **hands-on training** with **industry-standard tools**, ensuring students were job-ready from day one.
The institute also invested in **state-of-the-art infrastructure**, including green screen studios, editing suites, and soundproof recording rooms. These upgrades positioned FTIH as one of the few institutes in South India offering comprehensive media education under one roof.
### 🏆 Recognition and Awards (2015–2020)
FTIH’s commitment to excellence didn’t go unnoticed. Between **2018 and 2020**, the institute received several prestigious awards:
- **International Excellence Award** (2018–2019) for *Best Film School in South India*, presented by **Madhuri Dixit**.
- **Pride of Indian Education Award** (2019–2020), received from **Raveena Tandon**.
- **Most Popular Film School in South India**, awarded by **Brands Impact** and presented by **Dia Mirza**.
- Honored by **Sunil Shetty** as *South India’s Most Admired Film & Television Institute*.
These accolades reflected not just the quality of education, but the institute’s growing influence in shaping the next generation of filmmakers, editors, and performers.
## 🎥 FTIH Today: A Legacy of Creativity and Access
### 🌟 Vision and Leadership
Under the leadership of **Mr. Uday Kiran Katakam**, FTIH has continued to expand its offerings while staying true to its founding principles: **affordable, accessible, and industry-relevant education**. Mr. Katakam’s vision has always been to democratize media education—making it available to anyone with talent and drive.
FTIH’s faculty includes working professionals from the film and television industry, ensuring students receive **real-world insights** and **networking opportunities**. The institute also hosts **masterclasses** with industry legends like **J.D. Chakravarthy**, giving students firsthand exposure to cinematic craft.
### 🎓 Programs and Pedagogy
FTIH offers a wide range of programs:
- **Professional Courses**: Direction, Acting, Cinematography, Scriptwriting, Anchoring.
- **Technical Courses**: Film & TV Editing, VFX, Animation, Graphic & Web Design.
- **Short-Term Courses**: Tailored for quick skill development and employment.
The pedagogy emphasizes **project-based learning**, where students create short films, documentaries, and digital content as part of their coursework. This approach fosters creativity, collaboration, and confidence.
### 🌍 Impact and Alumni
FTIH alumni have gone on to work in **Tollywood**, **Bollywood**, and **digital media platforms**, contributing as directors, editors, actors, and technicians. The institute’s placement support and industry connections have helped many students launch successful careers.
Beyond professional success, FTIH has cultivated a **community of storytellers**—people who use media to express, challenge, and inspire. Its legacy is not just in the awards it has won, but in the voices it has empowered.
